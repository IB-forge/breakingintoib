Core Behavioral Questions

1. Tell me about yourself

Describe a time you failed and what you learned from it.

2. Why Investment Banking?

Discuss a situation where you had to work with a difficult team member.

3. Why our firm?

Share an example of a time you had to make a quick decision under pressure.

4. What are your strengths?

Describe a time you had to deal with a challenging client or customer.

5. What are your weaknesses?

Explain a situation where you had to adapt to a significant change.

6. Tell me about a time you failed

Discuss a time you had to lead a team through a difficult project.

7. Tell me about a time you had to work with a difficult team member

Share an example of a time you had to resolve a conflict within a team.

8. Tell me about a time you had to make a quick decision under pressure

Describe a time you had to persuade someone to see things your way.

9. Tell me about a time you had to deal with a challenging client or customer

Explain a situation where you had to go above and beyond to achieve a goal.

10. Tell me about a time you had to adapt to a significant change

Discuss a time you had to overcome a significant obstacle to succeed.

How to talk about your story

Crafting a compelling personal narrative is crucial for investment banking interviews. Structure your story to highlight relevant experiences and motivations, emphasizing key attributes sought by employers. Differentiate between impactful and forgettable answers by focusing on specific achievements and lessons learned, rather than generic descriptions. Use frameworks like STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) and CAR (Context, Action, Result) to ensure clarity and conciseness in your responses.

Resume Tips

Tailor your resume to highlight finance-related experiences and skills. Use action verbs to describe your accomplishments and quantify your results whenever possible.
