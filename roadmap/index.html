Investment Banking Career Roadmap

A structured, year-by-year plan for students in India, the US, and the UK, aspiring to enter Investment Banking.

Year 1

Goals: Build a strong academic foundation, explore finance-related fields, and develop foundational skills. Skills: Basic financial literacy, analytical thinking, communication skills. Internships: Shadowing opportunities, virtual internships, or part-time roles in finance-related startups. Mistakes to Avoid: Neglecting academics, focusing solely on technical skills, and not networking.

Year 2

Goals: Deepen understanding of financial markets, enhance technical skills, and gain practical experience. Skills: Financial modeling, valuation techniques, advanced Excel, presentation skills. Internships: Summer internships in boutique investment banks, financial consulting firms, or corporate finance departments. Mistakes to Avoid: Overemphasizing grades over practical experience, limiting internship search to large firms, and not seeking mentorship.

Year 3

Goals: Specialize in a specific area of investment banking, refine soft skills, and build a professional network. Skills: Industry-specific knowledge (e.g., M&A, equity research), leadership, negotiation, client relationship management. Internships: Competitive internships in top-tier investment banks, focusing on chosen specialization. Mistakes to Avoid: Lack of specialization, neglecting soft skills development, and not actively participating in industry events.

Final Year

Goals: Secure a full-time offer, prepare for interviews, and maintain academic excellence. Skills: Interview preparation, case study analysis, industry trends awareness, stress management. Internships: Final internships or graduate programs in target investment banks. Mistakes to Avoid: Underestimating interview preparation, not leveraging network, and settling for less than desired role.
